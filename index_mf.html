Mutual Fund Risk Analysis Tool

Explanation of Metrics

Standard Deviation: Indicates how volatile the fund's returns have been over the past 3 years. A lower value suggests more predictable performance. For example, if two funds have returned 9% in the last 3 years, the one with a lower standard deviation is likely to continue delivering similar returns.

Beta: Measures how volatile the fund's performance is compared to similar funds in the market. A lower beta implies more predictable performance. For example, if two funds have returned 9% in the last 3 years, the one with a lower beta is likely to continue delivering similar returns.

Sharpe Ratio: Indicates how much risk was taken to generate the returns. A higher value means the fund has delivered better returns for the amount of risk taken. It is calculated by subtracting the risk-free return (e.g., Indian Government Bond) from the fund’s returns and then dividing by the standard deviation of returns.

Treynor's Ratio: Indicates how much excess return was generated for each unit of risk taken. A higher value means the fund has delivered better returns for the amount of risk taken. It is calculated by subtracting the risk-free return from the fund’s returns and then dividing by the beta of returns.

Jensen's Alpha: Indicates how much additional return was generated compared to a benchmark. A positive alpha suggests the fund has outperformed the benchmark. For example, if a fund benchmarks its returns against Nifty50 and has an alpha of 1.0, it has beaten the Nifty returns by 1%.
